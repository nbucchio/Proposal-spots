How Much Does a Proposal in the Maldives Cost? (2026 Guide)

A Maldives proposal is really a resort decision, because the whole thing happens on one island and the villa is by far the biggest line in the budget. A four-night water villa stay with a proposal setup and a photographer starts at roughly $5,000 to $7,000 in shoulder season and runs $9,000 to $25,000 or more in peak at a premium resort. The proposal setup itself — a private sandbank, a dinner, a photographer — is only a small share of that. What actually moves the number is the resort tier, the season, and whether you reach the island by speedboat or seaplane.

There is one cost almost everyone forgets: a service charge and GST of around 23%, plus a small green tax, are added to nearly every bill. On a Maldives trip that is a four-figure line of its own, so it belongs in the budget from the start rather than as a surprise at checkout. What drives costs up

  • The resort tier. This is almost the entire decision. An ultra-luxury island (Soneva Jani, Joali, Cheval Blanc Randheli) at $2,000–$5,000 a night is a completely different budget from a near-Malé overwater resort at $400–$700 — for the same lagoon and the same sunset.
  • A seaplane-transfer resort. Farther atolls require a seaplane at roughly $350–$600 per person round-trip, and those resorts tend to charge more per night too. Near-Malé speedboat resorts avoid both.
  • Peak season (December–March, and holiday weeks). Villa rates rise 30–60% over shoulder and green season. The same water villa can double between May and February.
  • The 23% service charge and taxes. A service charge and GST of around 23%, plus a green tax, are added to nearly every bill. On a five-figure trip that is a four-figure line — the single most-forgotten Maldives cost.
  • Signature add-ons. A private sandbank charter, an underwater-restaurant dinner or a full floral setup are the extras that lift the setup from $1,500 toward $5,000.

Where people overspend

  • Not budgeting for the ~23% service charge. It is added to the villa, the dining and most extras, and it turns a $10,000 trip into a $12,000 one. Build it in from the start so it is a plan, not a checkout shock.
  • Choosing a far seaplane resort for the "remote" feel. A near-Malé speedboat resort delivers the same overwater villa and the same lagoon, arrives any time of day, and skips the seaplane fee entirely — often for a materially lower nightly rate.
  • Over-scaling the setup at an already-expensive resort. At an ultra-luxury island the setting is already the spectacle. A restrained sandbank setup photographs as beautifully as a maximal one, and the money is better left in the trip.
  • Booking peak holiday weeks. Christmas, New Year and Easter are the most expensive dates of the year. A shift into shoulder season is the biggest lever you have on the villa rate.

How to propose in the Maldives on a lower budget

  • Choose a near-Malé speedboat resort. The same overwater villa and lagoon, a cheaper nightly rate, no seaplane fee, and a flexible arrival time. This is the single biggest saving available in the Maldives.
  • Travel in the green or shoulder season (May–October). Villa rates drop sharply. You accept the chance of a passing shower in exchange for a materially smaller bill — and the lagoon is just as blue between them.
  • Use the resort's own photographer and setup. They know the tide windows and the best sandbank, and you avoid the cost and the permissions hassle of bringing someone in.
  • Keep the setup simple. The sandbank and the lagoon are the production. A clean setup at golden hour needs no more than flowers, a table and the light.
  • Budget the 23% upfront. Knowing the service charge is coming lets you pick the villa tier that lands your true all-in where you want it. You only need a full proposal package when you'd rather the resort handle every detail.

How much does a proposal in the Maldives cost?

A four-night water villa stay with a proposal setup and a photographer starts at roughly $5,000 to $7,000 in shoulder season and runs $9,000 to $25,000 or more in peak at a premium resort. A near-Malé speedboat resort can bring a full proposal trip closer to $3,000 to $4,000. The villa is the swing factor — the proposal setup itself is a small fraction of the total. Why is a Maldives proposal so much more than other destinations?

Because the proposal is inseparable from the resort. You are already paying $300 to $5,000 a night for an overwater villa, so the setup — a private sandbank, a dinner, a photographer, at $2,000 to $5,000 — is a small share of the trip. Then a 23% service charge and GST plus a green tax are added to nearly every bill, which is the cost most couples forget to budget for.

How much is a private sandbank proposal or setup?

The private sandbank charter is usually $600 to $2,500 depending on the resort and how far the sandbank sits from the lagoon. A full proposal package — the sandbank or beach setup, a private dinner and a photographer — typically runs $1,500 to $5,000. The resort delivers the setup ahead of you and you have roughly 90 minutes to two hours before the tide returns.

How much is a proposal photographer in the Maldives?

About $400 to $1,500 for a two-to-three-hour session, usually the resort's in-house photographer or one it works with regularly. If you want to bring an external photographer, confirm with the property first that they will be allowed onsite — some resorts restrict outside vendors. Seaplane or speedboat: which transfer is cheaper for a Maldives proposal?

Speedboat. Resorts near Malé reach by speedboat run any time of day and skip the seaplane fee; farther-atoll resorts require a seaplane at roughly $350 to $600 per person round-trip and only fly in daylight.
