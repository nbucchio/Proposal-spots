What Does a Proposal Actually Cost?

The most common question we get — answered honestly, destination by destination. We break down every cost category: the spot itself, photography, accommodation, setup, and the extras nobody mentions until it's too late.

How to use this guide: Every proposal is different. These ranges reflect real-world costs for each destination based on what couples actually spend. We separate "Just the Moment" costs (minimal) from "Hotel & Stay" costs (full experience). Always budget 15–20% above your target — destination proposals have a way of finding optional upgrades you'll want to say yes to.

How we choose spots

Most places are beautiful. Very few are right for a proposal.

Every location on Proposal Spots is evaluated against five criteria: genuine seclusion at the moment that matters, quality of light at golden hour, ease of access without logistical stress, protection from interruption, and the emotional weight of the space itself. Some places simply feel different when you're standing in them — a quality that no photograph fully captures, and no algorithm can assess.

We research each destination in person or through trusted local partners who know the difference between a location that looks impressive and one that will make her cry. We look at crowd patterns by hour and season. We eliminate anything with unpredictable access. We reject spots that require a perfect Instagram caption to justify them.

The majority of spots we scout don't make it. The ones on this platform are here because we would propose at them ourselves — and we hold them to exactly that standard.

The founder

Why I built
Proposal Spots.

My name is Nicholas Bucchio. In 2024, I began planning a proposal to the woman who is now my fiancée.

I knew what I wanted: something private, something beautiful, something that felt genuinely considered. Not a restaurant with a dessert menu. Not a tourist landmark at peak hour. A real place — somewhere that felt worthy of the question.

What I found, when I started researching, was chaos. Travel blogs with no real curation. Instagram posts without any practical information. Hotels that listed "proposal packages" but couldn't tell me where the actual moment would happen. A staggering amount of advice that told me almost nothing useful.

"I wanted to know: where exactly do I stand, at what time of day, and what will she see when she turns around? That information didn't exist anywhere in one place."

So I started building it myself. I researched locations obsessively. I contacted local photographers, guides, and hotel coordinators. I mapped out timing, crowd patterns, access routes, and golden hour windows. I eventually found the right place — and she said yes.

Proposal Spots exists because that information should have been easier to find. Because the most important question of your life deserves better than a Google search and a travel blog. Because the men who care enough to plan this right deserve a resource that meets them at that standard.

Every spot on this platform has been evaluated with the same obsessive criteria I used when planning my own proposal. If it wouldn't have been right for that moment, it isn't on this list.

— Nicholas Bucchio, Founder
