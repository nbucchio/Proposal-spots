Our Philosophy

Curation is
an act of care.

—

Atmosphere matters

The right setting creates an emotional state that no amount of planning can manufacture. We search for places that already carry weight — locations where the air feels different, where the light arrives perfectly, where the world seems to slow down.

—

Privacy is non-negotiable

A proposal is one of the most vulnerable moments a person will ever experience. It should never happen in a crowd. We prioritise locations and experiences that offer genuine seclusion — where the only witness is the person you love.

—

Details are the experience

The timing of arrival, the position of the sun, the distance to the nearest hotel — every detail changes the outcome. We document each one. Because we believe the person planning this moment deserves to feel completely prepared, and completely calm.

—

Curation over volume

We do not list everything. We list what we would genuinely recommend. Every spot on this platform has been reviewed for its emotional quality — not just its photography. If it does not move us, it does not make the list.

Our Process

From discovery
to the moment.

01

Discover the location

Browse our curated archive by destination, vibe, privacy, and budget. Every listing includes a full description, photo documentation, access details, and an honest assessment of what the experience actually feels like.

02

Select the proposal moment that matches your vision — whether that is a private jungle deck at sunset, a cliffside terrace above the sea, or an overwater villa at the end of the world. Then decide how much of the surrounding experience you want us to curate.

The founder

Why I built
Proposal Spots.

My name is Nicholas Bucchio. In 2024, I began planning a proposal to the woman who is now my fiancée.

I knew what I wanted: something private, something beautiful, something that felt genuinely considered. Not a restaurant with a dessert menu. Not a tourist landmark at peak hour. A real place — somewhere that felt worthy of the question.

What I found, when I started researching, was chaos. Travel blogs with no real curation. Instagram posts without any practical information. Hotels that listed "proposal packages" but couldn't tell me where the actual moment would happen. A staggering amount of advice that told me almost nothing useful.

"I wanted to know: where exactly do I stand, at what time of day, and what will she see when she turns around? That information didn't exist anywhere in one place."

So I started building it myself. I researched locations obsessively. I contacted local photographers, guides, and hotel coordinators. I mapped out timing, crowd patterns, access routes, and golden hour windows. I eventually found the right place — and she said yes.

Proposal Spots exists because that information should have been easier to find. Because the most important question of your life deserves better than a Google search and a travel blog. Because the men who care enough to plan this right deserve a resource that meets them at that standard.

Every spot on this platform has been evaluated with the same obsessive criteria I used when planning my own proposal. If it wouldn't have been right for that moment, it isn't on this list.

— Nicholas Bucchio, Founder
