# Stories — Proposal Spots

One founding story. Use it sparingly — once per post max, and only when it adds genuine weight to the content.

---

## The Founding Story

Nicholas Bucchio began planning a proposal in 2024 to the woman who is now his fiancée.

He knew what he wanted: somewhere private, somewhere beautiful, somewhere that felt genuinely considered. Not a restaurant dessert menu moment. Not a tourist landmark at peak hour.

What he found when he started researching was chaos. Travel blogs with no real curation. Instagram posts with no location data. Hotels listing "proposal packages" but unable to describe where the moment would actually happen.

He wanted to know one specific thing: where exactly do I stand, at what time of day, and what will she see when she turns around?

That information did not exist in one place.

So he built it himself. He researched obsessively. He contacted photographers, guides, hotel coordinators. He mapped golden hour windows, crowd patterns, access routes. He found the right place. She said yes.

Proposal Spots exists because that process should have been easier. Because the most important question of your life deserves better than a Google search and a travel blog.

---

## How to use this story

- Invoke it when establishing why curation matters
- Invoke it when explaining why specificity (time of day, crowd patterns, exact location) is a core feature — not a nice-to-have
- Do not use it as a feel-good opener — use it to support a point
- Never reproduce it in full in a blog post — extract the relevant detail only

## What this story proves

- The problem is real and personal
- The standard of curation comes from personal stakes, not editorial guidelines
- Every spot on the platform has been evaluated with the same criteria used for the founder's own proposal
