# Opinions — Proposal Spots

Strong positions the brand holds. Use one per post max. Always back with a reason or stat.

---

## Core opinions

**1. Most proposal advice is useless because it's not specific enough.**
"Go somewhere romantic" is not advice. Advice is: arrive at the terrace at 6:40pm in October, face northwest, the crowd thins after 6pm, the light is best from the left side of the path. Generic guidance exists because nobody has done the work. We do the work.

**2. The location matters as much as the ring — and nobody talks about it.**
Men spend months choosing a ring and 20 minutes choosing a location. The location is in every photo. It's in the story told for the rest of their lives. It deserves the same obsession.

**3. "Proposal packages" at hotels are mostly theater.**
A bottle of champagne on the bed and a card that says "Congratulations" is not a proposal experience. Most hotel packages exist to upsell a room night, not to create a meaningful moment. The good ones are rare. We only list those.

**4. Privacy is underrated in proposal planning.**
Most proposal guides recommend famous landmarks. Famous landmarks have crowds, other photographers, strangers watching. A private moment in a beautiful location beats a public performance every time — for the person proposing and the person being proposed to.

**5. The internet has made proposal planning harder, not easier.**
More content, less signal. Instagram posts with no location data. Affiliate travel blogs recommending places they've never visited. Reddit threads from 2019. The abundance of information has not produced better proposals — it has produced more anxiety and more generic outcomes.

**6. The best proposals feel inevitable, not staged.**
A great proposal location removes the need to perform. The setting does the work. You just have to show up, be present, and ask. That's what curation is actually for.

## How to use these

- State the opinion clearly and early — don't bury it
- Follow immediately with the reason or the evidence
- Don't soften it with "some might argue" — own the position
- One per post. Two makes the post feel like a rant.
