# Humour — Proposal Spots

## The role of humour here

This is not a comedy brand. It is an emotionally intelligent brand that uses humour strategically — to disarm, to build trust, and to acknowledge that planning a proposal is equal parts meaningful and nerve-wracking.

The reader is anxious. A well-placed observation that names that anxiety makes him feel understood. That's the function of humour here: connection, not entertainment.

---

## What works

**The honest observation**
Naming the absurd reality of the situation without mocking it.
> "You're about to make the most important ask of your life. Your current research strategy is a Reddit thread from 2019 and an Instagram photo with no location data."

**The understatement**
Describe something spectacular with deliberate restraint.
> "Santorini at sunset. A private terrace. Your person. No tourists in the frame. Low stakes."

**The self-aware aside (in parentheses)**
A brief wink to the reader that doesn't interrupt the flow.
> "The cliffside path takes about 20 minutes to walk. Bring water. (You will sweat. This is normal.)"

**The reframe**
Take something the reader is stressed about and reframe it with a light touch.
> "The good news: you don't need a plan. You need a location, a ring, and about four seconds of courage."

---

## What doesn't work

- Dad jokes — wrong brand register
- Puns on proposal/ring/yes — every travel blogger has done these
- Sarcasm — too sharp for the emotional weight of the subject
- Humour at the partner's expense — never
- Forced levity on serious topics (privacy, cost, safety) — read the room

---

## Frequency

One humorous moment per blog post. Maybe two if the topic is lighter.
Zero on any page that is primarily about price, safety, or accessibility.

---

## The test

Read the line aloud. If it makes you exhale with recognition — keep it. If it makes you wince even slightly — cut it.
