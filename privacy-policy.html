01

Introduction

Welcome to Proposal Spots. We are committed to protecting your personal information and being transparent about how we use it. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect when you visit proposalspots.com, why we collect it, how we use it, and the rights you have over it.

Proposal Spots is a curated discovery platform that helps people find unique proposal locations, romantic hotels, and destination engagement experiences worldwide. When you use our platform — browsing listings, submitting an enquiry, or contacting us — certain information may be collected in the ways described below.

This policy applies to all users of our website and is designed to comply with applicable privacy laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), UK GDPR, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), and the Australian Privacy Act 1988. Where your jurisdiction grants you specific rights, those are explained in Section 9.

Please read this policy carefully. By using proposalspots.com, you acknowledge the practices described here. If you have questions, you can reach us at contact@proposalspots.com at any time.

02

Who We Are

Proposal Spots operates proposalspots.com, a discovery platform connecting individuals with proposal venues, romantic accommodations, and engagement experience providers worldwide.

For the purposes of data protection law, Proposal Spots is the data controller — meaning we determine how and why your personal information is processed. Where we engage third-party service providers to process data on our behalf, those parties act as data processors under our instruction.

Contact details:

  • Website: proposalspots.com
  • Email: contact@proposalspots.com

If you are located in the EU or UK and have a privacy concern, you may contact us using the details in Section 13. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (generally 30 days).

03

Information We Collect

We collect information in two ways: information you give us directly, and information collected automatically when you use our site.

Information you provide to us

  • Name and email address — when you submit an enquiry about a listing, sign up for updates, or contact us via a form.
  • Enquiry details — any details you voluntarily include in a contact or enquiry form, such as travel dates, preferences, or questions.
  • Communications — if you email us directly, we retain that correspondence.

Information collected automatically

  • Location and search inputs — country, region, or city entered into search fields on our platform (used to return relevant results).
  • Usage and analytics data — pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URLs, and general browsing behaviour, collected through tools such as Google Analytics.
  • Device and technical data — browser type, operating system, screen resolution, IP address (used in aggregate or anonymised form).
  • Cookie and tracking data — data collected via cookies and similar technologies as described in Section 7.

Information we do not collect

We do not knowingly collect sensitive personal data (such as health information, financial account credentials, or government ID numbers). We do not collect payment card details directly — any future payment functionality will be handled by a certified third-party payment processor.

04

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries — to reply to questions, provide information about specific listings, and follow up on requests you have made.
  • Connecting you with partners — when you enquire about a specific venue or experience, we may share your enquiry details with the relevant partner to facilitate your request (see Section 6).
  • Improving the platform — to understand how visitors use Proposal Spots, identify popular content, fix issues, and enhance the overall experience.
  • Marketing communications — with your consent, we may send you occasional emails featuring curated destinations, inspiration, or platform updates. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in any email or by contacting us directly.
  • Analytics and performance — to measure site traffic, track aggregate usage patterns, and improve platform performance using tools such as Google Analytics.
  • Legal compliance — to comply with applicable laws, respond to lawful requests from authorities, or enforce our terms of service.

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Proposal Spots uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to provide core functionality, measure performance, and (in future) support marketing. When you first visit our site, a cookie consent banner will appear allowing you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can update your preferences at any time.

A cookie is a small text file placed on your device by a website. Cookies help the site remember your preferences and understand how you use it. They are not harmful and cannot access other files on your device.

Managing your cookie preferences

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Cookie banner — use the consent banner that appears on your first visit to accept or decline non-essential cookies.
  • Browser settings — most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or delete existing ones. Note that disabling essential cookies may affect site functionality.
  • Google Analytics opt-out — you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law.

  • Enquiry data (name, email, enquiry content) — retained for up to 2 years from the date of the enquiry, unless you request earlier deletion or an ongoing relationship requires longer retention.
  • Marketing email list — retained until you unsubscribe or request removal.
  • Analytics data — retained in line with Google Analytics' default data retention settings (up to 26 months for aggregated data).
  • Cookie consent records — retained for up to 12 months to demonstrate your consent choices.
  • Legal and compliance records — retained for as long as required by applicable law.

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it. You may request early deletion at any time — see Section 9.

Your Rights

Depending on where you are located, you have specific legal rights over your personal data. We take these rights seriously and will respond to all valid requests promptly — in most cases within 30 days. There is no charge for exercising your rights, unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive.

To submit a request, email us at contact@proposalspots.com with your name and a clear description of your request. We may ask you to verify your identity before acting on sensitive requests.

GDPR & UK GDPR — EU, UK, Germany, Switzerland

Your rights under GDPR and UK GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Germany, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and its UK equivalent:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — you can ask us to delete your personal data where it is no longer necessary, or where you withdraw consent and there is no other lawful basis for processing.
  • Right to data portability — where processing is based on consent or contract, you may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format to transfer to another service.
  • Right to restriction of processing — you can ask us to pause processing of your data in certain circumstances (e.g., while accuracy is disputed).
  • Right to object — you can object to processing based on our legitimate interests at any time. We will cease processing unless we have compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent (e.g., marketing emails), you may withdraw at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — you have the right to complain to your local data protection authority. In Germany: bfdi.bund.de. In the UK: ico.org.uk. In Switzerland: edoeb.admin.ch.
CCPA — California, United States

Your rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) grant you the following rights:

  • Right to know — you may request details about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources, the business purposes, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to delete — you may request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., completing a transaction, legal obligations).
  • Right to correct — you may request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing — Proposal Spots does not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising. You therefore do not need to opt out, but we will honour any such request.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information — we do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by the CPRA.
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

To submit a verifiable consumer request, email contact@proposalspots.com. We will respond within 45 days, with a possible 45-day extension where reasonably necessary.

PIPEDA — Canada

Your rights under PIPEDA

If you are located in Canada, Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) governs how we handle your data. Your rights include:

  • Right of access — you may request access to the personal information we hold about you, the purposes for which it is used, and the third parties to whom it has been disclosed.
  • Right to correction — you may request that inaccurate or incomplete personal information be corrected.
  • Right to withdrawal of consent — you may withdraw consent to our collection, use, or disclosure of your personal information at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions. Note that withdrawal may limit our ability to provide certain services.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — you may file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada at priv.gc.ca.

We collect only the minimum personal information necessary, use it only for the identified purpose, and obtain meaningful consent before or at the time of collection.

Australian Privacy Act 1988

Your rights under the Australian Privacy Act

If you are located in Australia, the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) apply to how we handle your personal information. Your rights include:

  • Right of access — you may request access to the personal information we hold about you. We will respond within 30 days and provide the information in a requested format where reasonable.
  • Right to correction — if you believe information we hold is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant, or misleading, you may ask us to correct it. We will respond within 30 days.
  • Right to anonymity — where lawful and practicable, you have the option to interact with us without identifying yourself.
  • Right to complain — if you believe we have breached the APPs, you may first complain to us directly. If unresolved, you may escalate to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

We handle Australian users' personal information in accordance with the APPs and will not use it for a purpose other than the primary purpose of collection without consent, unless an exception applies.

Unsubscribing from marketing

Regardless of your location, you may opt out of marketing emails at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link included in any email we send, or by emailing contact@proposalspots.com. We will process your request promptly and you will receive no further marketing communications. Transactional emails (e.g., replies to an active enquiry) may still be sent as they are necessary to fulfil your request.

International Data Transfers

Proposal Spots operates globally and may transfer your personal data to countries outside your home jurisdiction, including countries that may not provide the same level of data protection as your own.

Where we transfer data from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to third countries, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • The use of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission or UK equivalent.
  • Transfers to countries with an adequacy decision by the relevant authority.
  • Other legally recognised transfer mechanisms as applicable.

For example, Google Analytics may process data on servers in the United States. Google LLC participates in data transfer frameworks that provide appropriate safeguards for such transfers.

You may request information about the specific safeguards applicable to any transfer of your data by contacting us at contact@proposalspots.com.

Children's Privacy
